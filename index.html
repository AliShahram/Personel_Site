About Me

Besides coding, I love poetry and soccer. My interest in coding comes from a passion in mathematics and problem-solving. I enjoy the process of studying a problem, brainstorming and coming up with different algorithms until I reach the most optimal solution. As a developer, I don’t admire the traditional role of sitting in a cubicle and coding 9 to 5. I am rather passionate to push my technical skills with people who share the same drive to solving real-world problems through collaboration and creative problem-solving.

A year ago, after observing the challenges facing the transportation system in Richmond, IN, I created a solution to help the system not only become more efficient but also to better meet customer needs. Based on this concept I was awarded a $5,000 grant from Earlham College’s Prize for Creative Capitalism. I recruited and led a team that developed two mobile applications and a corresponding website. The new system better-connected clients to transportation options and offered online tracking, booking, and payment. To keep us on task, I set intermediate goals and promoted effective communication. Together, we ensured the integrity of application through analysis, coding and writing extensive documentation. As a result, the city of Richmond has predicted a 40% or higher increase in ridership and over $300,000 in additional yearly revenue.

For the past years, I have gained working experience of full-stack development as part of teams of different sizes, and objectives in the US, UK and Afghanistan. With each team, I’ve familiarized myself with the usual tools and programming styles within the organizations and learned to thrive by practicing adaptability and taking on different roles and responsibilities while staying dedicated to our shared goals and deadlines.

Thank you for your time, and I am looking forward to connecting with you in the future.
